The Problem We Solve

Public hospitals are overcrowded.
The information gap makes it worse.

Patients arrive without knowing wait times. Doctors have no real-time workload view. Administrators can't see bottlenecks as they form.

❌ Without Chikitsaalye
⏳ Patients wait 45–90 minutes without knowing if the doctor is even available
🗒 Doctors receive patients without prior context, repeating the same questions
📋 Paperwork is manual, misplaced, or duplicated across departments
🔀 Handoffs between OPD, lab, pharmacy, and ward happen verbally or on paper slips
🏥 Administrators have no visibility into patient flow until it's already a problem
📞 Referrals to other departments get lost or never closed
✅ With Chikitsaalye
📲 Patients see real-time queue position, estimated wait, and doctor room number before arriving
💻 Doctors open consultations with patient context, past visits, and pending tasks already visible
🔁 Structured workflows replace paper slips for referrals, discharge, and lab collection
📊 Administrators see department load, wait trends, and discharge blockers in real time
🔔 Alerts surface only when action is needed — not noise, only signal
📁 Reports, prescriptions, and summaries are accessible digitally by the patient

Our Mission

A public-health coordination layer for India

Chikitsaalye is not a telemedicine platform and not a private healthcare product. It is a coordination infrastructure designed specifically for government hospitals, district medical colleges, and public-health systems in India.

The system is built to operate under the real constraints of Indian public health — low bandwidth, multilingual patient populations, heavy OPD loads, limited staffing, and complex inter-departmental workflows that paper-based systems cannot support at scale.

Our goal is to reduce the gap between patients who need care and the clinical staff and infrastructure that can provide it — by making status, next steps, and pending actions visible to everyone who needs to act on them.
